1 Before You Start

You'll be testing the live deployed app — no setup, no Flutter, no local environment needed. Just open it in your browser:

App URL: https://erams-98eb2.web.app/

Because the dispatcher, driver, hospital, and admin actions need to happen at the same time to see the real-time updates, you need four separate logged-in sessions open simultaneously. The app stores your login session in the browser, so the same browser can only hold one session at a time.

Open four separate browser windows, all pointed at the link above — e.g. one normal window plus three Incognito/Private windows (Ctrl+Shift+N in Chrome), or four different browsers (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Brave). Sign in to a different test account in each one and arrange them side by side.

3 Core Test Flow — Full Incident Lifecycle

Tip: only UBE 001A has a driver account linked to it. Drop your Step 1 incident pin near Nakasero (central Kampala) so auto-dispatch picks UBE 001A automatically — or use the Manual assignment option in Step 2 and pick UBE 001A directly. See Seeded Reference Data below for the full ambulance/hospital list.

Follow these steps in order, switching between your four browser windows as indicated. Each step says which role performs it and what you should see happen.

Step 1 Dispatcher Log a new incident
  • Sign in as the Dispatcher account.
  • Click "New Incident".
  • Drop a pin on the map near Nakasero (central Kampala).
  • Fill in nature of emergency, reporter name/phone, patient condition notes.
  • Set the hospital to Mulago National Referral Hospital.
  • Submit the form.
Expected: the new incident appears instantly as a card and a red map marker, with status LOGGED — no page refresh needed.
Step 2 Dispatcher Dispatch the nearest ambulance
  • On the new incident card, click "Dispatch Nearest".
  • If a different ambulance is chosen automatically, click "Manual" instead and assign UBE 001A directly.
Expected: the card updates to DISPATCHED with UBE 001A shown as the assigned ambulance, with no page refresh.
Step 3 Driver Receive the alert and head out
  • Switch to the Driver window (already signed in).
  • Confirm the active incident card appears automatically — this is the real-time alert, no refresh needed.
  • Try the "Navigate to Scene" button — it should open Google Maps in a new tab with the incident location pre-loaded as the destination and driving mode selected.
  • Click "I'm En Route".
Expected: the Dispatcher's card updates to EN ROUTE and the ambulance marker colour changes on the map, live.
Step 4 Hospital See the incoming patient and acknowledge
  • Switch to the Hospital window (already signed in as the Mulago account).
  • Confirm the incident appears under "Incoming" with the patient condition notes and a live ETA.
  • Click "Acknowledge — Ready to Receive".
  • Refresh the page and confirm the button still shows "Acknowledged" (this was a bug we fixed — it must survive a refresh).
Expected: the ETA updates live as the driver's GPS position changes; acknowledgement persists after refresh.
Step 5 Driver Arrive and complete the incident
  • Back in the Driver window, click "I've Arrived".
  • Then click "Incident Complete".
Expected: the incident disappears from the Dispatcher's active list, and UBE 001A returns to Available status.
Step 6 Dispatcher + Hospital + Driver Check history tabs
  • In each of the three windows, open the "History" tab.
Expected: the completed incident appears in the Dispatcher's, Hospital's, and Driver's history list.

4 Admin Module Checklist

Switch to the Admin window for these checks.

Fleet tab
  • Confirm all 5 seeded ambulances are listed with status badges.
  • Click "Add Ambulance" — create a test ambulance, assign a driver/hospital, save.
  • Edit an existing ambulance's assigned driver and confirm it persists after refresh.
Users tab
  • Confirm all 4 demo accounts are listed with readable names (not "?" avatars).
  • Click "Add User", fill in a test account, submit — confirm a one-time temporary password is shown.
  • Sign in as that new account with the temp password in a fresh window — confirm it forces you to set your own password before reaching the dashboard.
  • On an existing user, open the ⋮ menu → "Edit Details" — change name/phone, save, refresh to confirm it persisted.
  • On an existing user, open ⋮ → "Reset Password", confirm, then sign in as that user with the new temp password — confirm the forced password-change screen appears again.
  • Tap a role badge and change a user's role — confirm it updates immediately.
Analytics tab
  • Confirm the total incident count matches what you'd expect from testing so far.
  • Confirm the Avg Response Time KPI reflects the incident you completed in Step 5.
  • Confirm the "incidents by hospital" and "incidents by status" charts render without errors.

5 Other Checks

  • Profile sheet — on every role, click the profile icon in the top app bar, edit your name/phone, save, confirm it persists.
  • Sign out — confirm the logout icon returns you to the Login screen on every role.
  • Responsive layout — resize the browser window narrower (or open on a phone) and confirm the Dispatcher/Admin views remain usable, and the Driver/Hospital views look right on a mobile width.
  • Android driver app — if an APK build is available, install it on a physical device and repeat Steps 3 and 5 from a real phone to confirm live GPS tracking works outside the browser.

7 Known Caveats — Not Bugs

  • On Flutter web, you may see a console warning/assertion related to window.dart when GPS starts — this is a known Flutter web/geolocator quirk and does not affect functionality. GPS only matters on the Android driver app for a real demo.
  • The hospital ETA is a straight-line distance ÷ average speed estimate, not a real routing calculation — times will be optimistic compared to real driving time.
